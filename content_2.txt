
Far out at sea, a ship cut through the waves like a knife, its sails taut with the force of the wind. The crew moved with precision, every motion practiced and purposeful. Below deck, maps and instruments lay scattered across a wooden table, illuminated by the flicker of an oil lamp. The captain stared out at the horizon, a weathered journal in hand, charting a course few dared to follow.

The marketplace buzzed with life as vendors called out their wares—spices, silks, trinkets, and toys—each stall a burst of color and scent. Musicians strummed lutes and beat drums, adding rhythm to the clamor. Children weaved through crowds with sticky fingers and wide eyes, while old women haggled fiercely over the price of ginger. The smell of grilled meat and sweet pastries hung thick in the air.
